Preliminary Round

Foundational Knowledge Accumulation

3 Months Online Self-Study

As the starting point of the competition, the Preliminary Round is a "Foundational Knowledge Bootcamp" designed for all students with a basic vision. For a registration fee of just $100, students gain exclusive access to a full set of high-value educational resources on the YLMUN official website, including lecture videos from experts in international affairs and learning materials tailored to different levels.

Spanning 3 months, this phase features modular, thematic online courses that systematically break down the UN's organizational structure and delve into core global governance topics. Complemented by targeted self-paced assignments and a final course assessment, it transforms students' theoretical knowledge into solid academic expertise. Whether students are Model UN novices or experienced participants, this stage lays the first firm foundation from "understanding the UN" to "mastering core issues," preparing participants for the National Round.

Day 2- Competition Schedule

Session Detailed Arrangements Important Notes
Participant Registration Check-in by grade and category, verification of credentials, and equipment debugging. Please arrive punctually with valid identification documents.
Opening Ceremony Address by the organizer, jury panel introduction, and competition rule explanation. All participants must be in attendance.
Group Speech Competition The competition is conducted by division. Each participant delivers a 3-minute themed speech. Timing will be strictly enforced; points are deducted for exceeding limits.
Break Time Grouping Session Speech score announcement, debate grouping draw, and collection of debate guidelines. The grouping results are final and cannot be changed.
Debate Competition Team debate with speaking order, timed argument rounds, and free Q&A session. The debate topic is determined by on-site drawing of lots.
Lunch Break Buffet lunch service and exhibition of outstanding previous works. Please keep the venue clean.
Model UN Resolution Restore the UN agenda process and draft resolutions around key global issues. Please keep quiet and respect other participants.
Judges' Deliberation Judges hold closed-door deliberation and determine the final award list. Participants may wait in the designated rest area.

Detailed Assessment Syllabus for the National Round

Speech Competition (English)

Topic: The UN Sustainable Development Goals and Our Lives

Requirement: The topic will be announced at the time of registration, and students shall prepare independently.

Debate Competition (English)

Grouping Guidelines: After the speech competition, judges randomly group participants into three tiers (A, B, C) based on their speech scores. Groupings will be announced once all scores are finalized. The competition is divided into three categories: Primary School, Junior High School, and Senior High School.

Team Structure:

  • 3v3 or 4v4 format (10-12 teams)
  • If the number of participants is odd, a 3v4 format may be used.
Model UN Resolution (English)

In this session, we will cover the fundamental knowledge of the United Nations and fully restore the official UN agenda and procedures. Discussions will be conducted in strict accordance with authentic UN meeting processes around core topics including:

  • Sustainable Development
  • Environmental Protection
  • Social Development and more.
